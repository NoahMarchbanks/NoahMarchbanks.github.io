Reflection

As a second semester college student, I have already learned so much more than what I could’ve imagined. I started my college experience as a Computer Science major and after my first semester, decided that there was a program that the IT program fit me more comfortably. Although computer science is a great program and had tons to offer me, as things started to drift further away from what I originally wanted, I had to take a step back and ask myself what my goals were. I decided to go with IT because it is more my pace and focuses on the things that I value the most in my academic career.

During college, I have been able to learn a lot about myself. I have realized what I like to learn about and the things that I am best at. I have also learned to work not only on the things I am already good at, but especially the things that I’m not the strongest in. This allows me to address my weaknesses and eventually turn them into strengths.

The beautiful program of Information Technology has given me the opportunity to learn the things I am most interested in and allows me to hone my skills in every aspect imaginable. I am able to focus not only on software development, but things such as audio and graphic design. These different departments can be combined into every project that I work on in my future career to produce better and more efficient results.

I have learned so much about coding in only two semesters and plan to continue my journey, learning just about anything I can get my hands on. I love to learn and use my abilities to create things for the world and aspire to be able to continue doing so throughout my life after college as a software developer.
